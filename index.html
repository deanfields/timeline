Timeline

World Wide Web Opens to the Public

Tim Berners-Lee, an English scientist, invented the World Wide Web and released it to the public on October 24th 1991. The first image shared was of the CERN house band, Les Horribles Cernettes and today the web is a major part of everyday life for billions.

  • Date: 1991
  • Websites Launched: 1
  • Internet Users: 0

Ebay was founded

From his front room in San Jose, Pierre Omidyar founded eBay. It was initially meant as a place for individuals to sell goods and services but has now become a multi-billion dollar online marketplace for both business and individuals.

  • Date: 1995
  • Websites Launched: 23,500
  • Internet Users: 44,838,900

Email Surpasses Postal Mail

Just 4 years after the launch of the World Wide Web it was announced that email usage had officially surpassed postal mail. The achievement was largely down to both computers and the Internet now being accessible to the masses.

  • Date: 1996
  • Websites Launched: 257,601
  • Internet Users: 77,433,860

Nokia 6110 mobile phone launched

The Nokia 6610 started a range of classic business phones that saw loyal brand adherents for nearly 10 years after its release. The phone was smaller and lighter than anything Nokia had produced to date.

  • Date: 1997
  • Websites Launched: 1,117,255
  • Internet Users: 120,758,310

Google becomes a privately held company

Larry Page and Sergey Brin registered Google as a privately held company with the goal of making the world’s information more accessible. An initial public offering was made in 2004 and Google moved to new offices later that year.

  • Date: 1998
  • Websites Launched: 2,410,067
  • Internet Users: 188,023,930

First Blackberry handset released

The Blackberry 850 was launched in 1999 as Blackberry’s first mobile device to integrate email. The device was well received and led to Blackberry’s dominance of the sector over the next few years.

  • Date: 1999
  • Websites Launched: 3,177,453
  • Internet Users: 280,866,670

Amstrad Emailer came out

Essentially a telephone with a LCD screen and limited dialup and email capabilities, the Amstrad Emailer was released in 2000. The product was controversial as frequent checking of mail was expensive.

  • Date: 2000
  • Websites Launched: 17,087,182
  • Internet Users: 413,425,190

Wikipedia was founded

Rick Gates made the earliest proposal of an online encyclopaedia in 1993, but it was in 2001 when Jimmy Wales and Larry Singer were able to combine the world’s information into Wikipedia and launch it for all to use.

  • Date: 2001
  • Websites Launched: 29,254,370
  • Internet Users: 413,425,190

Facebook created

Along with the help of his 4 college friends, Mark Zuckerberg launched Facebook from his Harvard dorm room. Originally released exclusively to students at Harvard, today Facebook is accessible to the world and has over 1 billion members.

  • Date: 2004
  • Websites Launched: 51,611,646
  • Internet Users: 910,060,180

65% of Adults own mobile phones

Due to handsets becoming increasingly accessible, 65% of adults now own mobile phones. This number would later grow to 91% in 2013 and show little signs of slowing down.

  • Date: 2004
  • Websites Launched: 29,254,370
  • Internet Users: 413,425,190

Twitter created

After endless brainstorming sessions and 5 months of flat out coding and design work, Jack Dorsey and his team launched Twitter. The service rapidly gained worldwide popularity and is still a major force within the social media industry.

  • Date: 2006
  • Websites Launched: 85,507,314
  • Internet Users: 1,160,335,280

Apple release first iPhone

The Apple iPhone completely revolutionised the mobile phone industry. The first generation iPhone saw 6.1 million units sold worldwide and has been succeeded by a wealth of extremely popular upgraded iPhone handsets since 2007.

  • Date: 2007
  • Websites Launched: 121,892,559
  • Internet Users: 1,373,327,790

Google Nexus 1 announced

The Google Nexus 1 was Google’s first Nexus smartphone and the company’s first major offering in the mobile phone handset market. The device was praised for its processor, design and display.

  • Date: 2010
  • Websites Launched: 206,956,723
  • Internet Users: 2,045,865,660

4 day Blackberry outage costs $54mil

For four days Blackberry users could only send texts and make calls. Data wasn’t flowing correctly to the phones so access to Internet related apps was cut off. Over 70 million users were affected and it cost Blackberry $54mil.

  • Date: 2011
  • Websites Launched: 346,004,403
  • Internet Users: 2,282,955,130

New Twitter record - peak of 327,452 tweets per minute during USA Elections

As the results of the 2012 US Elections were announced, Twitter saw a peak of 357,452 tweets per minute. Obama’s celebratory tweet has been retweeted over 700,000 times and counting.

  • Date: 2012
  • Websites Launched: 697,089,489
  • Internet Users: 2,518,453,530

Edward Snowden releases over 1.7 million classifed records from NASA

American privacy activist and former CIA employee, Edward Snowden leaked classified information from the US NSA. The information revealed many top-secret global surveillance operations.

  • Date: 2012
  • Websites Launched: 672,985,183
  • Internet Users: 2,756,198,420

WhatsApp sees 50 billion messages per day, more than all SMS combined

With WhatsApp expanding to a point where they have 430 million active users, the world’s largest mobile messaging app announced that it now sees 50 billion messages sent per day through the service.

  • Date: 2014
  • Websites Launched: 968,882,453
  • Internet Users: 2,925,249,355*

91% of adults own mobile phones

Up from 65% in 2004, in 2013 it was announced that 91% of adults own a mobile phone. The mobile phone is the most quickly adopted consumer technology in the history of the world.

  • Date: 2014
  • Websites Launched: 968,882,453
  • Internet Users: 2,925,249,355*

Apple sells record 13 million new iPhones

Just 3 days after the official launch of the latest iPhone models, the 6s and the 6s Plus, Apple announced that it had sold a record 13 million new iPhones.

  • Date: 2014
  • Websites Launched: 968,882,453
  • Internet Users: 3,247,404,102
